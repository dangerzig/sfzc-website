Located at 300 Page Street in San Francisco, our 100-year-old building was designed by pioneering California architect Julia Morgan and originally housed young Jewish women making their way in the big city. Purchased by SFZC in the 60s, it has been a Soto Zen Buddhist Temple ever since. In 2024, the building went through an extensive, year-long renovation that included new bathrooms, a lovely Welcome Center, and an elevator.

There are several ways to stay at City Center/Beginner’s Mind Temple:

[Overnight Guests

Visitors to SF are welcome to reserve a guest room at City Center for a unique experience. Guests are welcome (but not required) to join the community in Zen practice.](https://www.sfzc.org/locations/city-center/visits-stays-city-center/guest-accommodations-city-center)

[Guest Students

**Guest students** can participate in the practice schedule and daily life of City Center for a period of 2–6 weeks. Learn more here.](https://www.sfzc.org/locations/city-center/visits-stays-city-center/guest-student-stays-city-center)

[Apprenticeships

For longer stays, City Center offers several **apprenticeships** lasting from three months up to two years. Learn more about apprenticeships here.](https://www.sfzc.org/locations/city-center/visits-stays-city-center/apprenticeship-programs-city-center)

**Programs & Events**

City Center also offers many [onsite and online programs and events](https://www.sfzc.org/calendar).
