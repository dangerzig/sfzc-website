> Let things come home to your heart,  
> and let your heart go out and abide in things.  
> —Zen Master Dogen

*Four Saturdays, May 2–Saturday, May 23, 10:30 am–12:00 pm PT*

Food is mystery, source, and sustenance. Zen Master Tenkei said, “See with your eyes, smell with your nose, taste with your tongue…nothing in the universe is hidden. What else would you have me say?”

Beyond recipes and rules, the kitchen offers liberation. When you let ingredients come forward to awaken and nourish, you discover a freer way to cook, and your own body and mind come alive with it.

Suzuki Roshi's teaching was, "When you are cooking you're not just cooking, you're working on yourself, you're working on others."

In this online class, we will study Dogen’s *Tenzokyokun* (or *Instructions to the Head Cook*), drawing on Ed Brown’s personal experience serving as Tassajara’s first tenzo; Suzuki Roshi’s teachings, both anecdotal and written; and other stories and teachings. For those who’d like to go further, students may also wish to read Ed’s book *No Recipe: Cooking as Spiritual Practice* (2018) and/or view Doris Dörrie’s film *How to Cook Your Life* (2007).
