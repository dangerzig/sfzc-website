Description

This eight-month, online program is geared towards students who have an established practice and basic understanding of Zen. It offers a broad sweep of Buddhist teachings, beginning with early Theravada and continuing through the Mahayana and Zen. Each month we will explore a different topic, including: The Three Characteristics, Dependent Origination, Emptiness and the Heart Sutra, The Two Truths, Mind and Awareness, Suchness, Buddha Nature, and Awakening.

This program is not intended for those who are entirely new to practice. Diving Deep into Zen complements our introductory programs [Establishing the Path of Practice](https://www.sfzc.org/calendar/events/online/establishing-path-practice-part-2-online-107-1125) and [Meditation Training 1.0](https://www.sfzc.org/calendar/events/city-center/meditation-training-10-cc-18-129) and [2.0](https://www.sfzc.org/calendar/events/city-center/meditation-training-20-cc-25-319), but equivalent experience is acceptable, and participation in these prior courses is not required to register.

Each session will weave meditation, a Dharma talk, and interactive inquiry to explore and deepen our understanding. The main Thursday sessions present material through a Dharma talk and include interactive Inquiry and discussion. The second Thursday Inquiry sessions provide an expanded opportunity for further exploration of the topic. There will also be opportunities for small peer discussion groups, arranged to best match the schedules of participants.

After each session, you will receive readings, reflections, and practices to support the integration and embodiment of the teaching topics in everyday life. Access to recorded talks, readings, and assignments for the program will be available through a dedicated program portal.

**Criteria for Registration**

This program is intended for those who have an established practice, including a daily meditation practice and an understanding of basic Buddhist teachings. Because of the interactive aspect of the program, we ask for a strong commitment to the precepts and a commitment to engaging the program as fully as possible.
