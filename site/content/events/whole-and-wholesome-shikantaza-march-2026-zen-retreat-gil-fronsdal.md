Description

This two-week retreat offers an opportunity for experienced meditators to join the Green Gulch staff in a period of intensive residential practice. The teaching and practice orientation will be "Shikantaza Through the Support of Insight." Our schedule will include morning, afternoon, and evening zazen; formal oryoki meals; work periods; Dharma talks; classes; and group discussions. There will also be formal and informal meetings with teachers. Participants commit to not leave the Green Gulch watershed for the duration (except by arrangement or emergencies). The retreat includes a five-day sesshin.

**To Apply:**

Please review the [Sample Schedule/Shingi](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1jldjptbN0KIWqaOjRSNLrWMCKHndnuUO/edit?tab=t.0) before submitting your application.

Prerequisite: At least two all-day sits, a residential sesshin, and/or a guest student stay at Green Gulch. The prerequisite for practitioners without a background in Zen practice is two weeklong silent residential meditation retreats.

If you have questions about the Zen Retreat and aren't sure if applying is right for you based on your practice experience, please contact the Abbot's assistant at [ggfabbotassist@sfzc.org](mailto:ggfabbotassist@sfzc.org) before applying.

Complete and submit an [intensive application](https://www.tfaforms.com/5190390).

Please contact [ggfabbotassist@sfzc.org](mailto:ggfabbotassist@sfzc.org) if you have any questions, or if you require early acceptance notification for travel.
