This class is offered as a bridge between the language of recovery and the language of Buddhist practice. Each expresses truth through a different doorway, one through the Twelve Steps and the other through the Dharma. The purpose is not to merge belief systems but to show how both paths speak to the same experience of awakening, healing, and service.

These parallels will be woven throughout the six-week class as part of meditation, reflection, and group dialogue. Participants are encouraged to engage with the material as personal exploration rather than doctrine.

### **Course Outline**

**Week 1 – The Turning Point: Willingness and Surrender**  
Theme: Recognizing the moment of readiness, the crack where light enters.

**Week 2 – Living by Vow: From Control to Intention**  
Theme: Replacing old survival strategies with the clarity of vow.

**Week 3 – Meditation as Medicine**  
Theme: Stillness as healing; awareness as detox.

**Week 4 – Sangha and the Healing Power of Community**  
Theme: From isolation to connection, the sangha and the rooms of recovery as mirror and refuge.

**Week 5 – Self-Compassion and the Middle Way**  
Theme: Healing the inner critic and learning to trust your own goodness.

**Week 6 – People, Places, and Things: Walking the Path in Daily Life**  
Theme: Integrating recovery and practice into everyday living.
